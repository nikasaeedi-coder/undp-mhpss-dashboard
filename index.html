THE GLOBAL FIELD: What Others Are Building — And What UNDP Has Adopted
UNDP does not work alone. This section maps the innovation models in the MHPSS field — those UNDP originated, those it has adopted, and those developed by the field that represent scaling opportunities. Understanding this landscape is key to positioning UNDP's comparative advantage.

🌍 Field-Developed Models — UNDP Scaling Opportunities

These models are proven in the field by partners. UNDP has the mandate, presence in fragile contexts, and SP 2026-2029 alignment to adopt and scale these approaches — particularly in settings where other actors are not present.

Key Sustainability Lessons

Duration Matters

MHPSS projects need 3-5 years minimum, not the typical 12-18 months. The PBF GYPI evaluation called for this explicitly.

Government Embedding

The Gambia validated a new national mental health policy. Burundi police allocated internal budgets to continue MHPSS. Institutional anchoring is key.

Triple Integration

Combining MHPSS + peacebuilding + livelihoods produces outcomes none can achieve alone. Rwanda's 40% poverty reduction proves this.

Funding Cliff Risk

Burundi's strong results (95% improved incomes) stalled when funding ended. Somalia community workers felt abandoned. Plan for sustainability from day one.

Mainstream, Don't Silo

The PBF evaluation found MHPSS should be mainstreamed across all peacebuilding projects rather than treated as standalone programming.

Youth as Agents

Kyrgyzstan, Bangladesh, and Denmark prove young people are effective agents of psychosocial change when positioned as co-designers, not just beneficiaries.

WHERE UNDP CAN SCALE: Strategic Opportunities Aligned with SP 2026-2029
UNDP's comparative advantage is its presence in fragile and polycrisis settings where brain economy gains remain out of reach. The four opportunity areas below directly map to SP 2026-2029 objectives and represent where UNDP can advance, deepen, and lead.

How UNDP Can Advance, Scale, and Do More

Based on UNDP's documented impact, the global field landscape, and SP 2026-2029 objectives, four strategic scaling opportunities emerge. Each builds on what UNDP has already demonstrated — and where the global need is greatest.

🔵

Scale the Brain Economy Equity Agenda

SP Obj 1 + 3 | SP Accelerator: Digital + Finance

The gap: The Brain Economy and Brain Capital conversations (WEF, OECD, G20) are dominated by stable, middle/high-income contexts. Fragile and crisis-affected countries are largely absent from the global framework-setting.

UNDP's unique position: UNDP is the only multilateral development actor with a specific mandate and field presence in fragile contexts that has already contributed to the global brain economy agenda — co-hosting the "Brain-Positive Innovations in Crisis Recovery" session with EMEA & Rice University (Sep 2025) and participating in the UNGA80 Brain Economy Summit. The next step is formalising this positioning.

Concrete opportunity: Develop 3–5 "brain economy investment cases for fragile contexts" (modelled on the Philippines mental health investment case) to demonstrate that the brain economy is not the privilege of stability. Position UNDP as the lead voice on equity in the brain economy globally.

SP alignment: Obj 1 (Prosperity for All) — expanding economic capabilities through mental health investment; Sustainable Finance Accelerator (B.3) — brain economy as new financing frame for MHPSS.
🌿

Mainstream MHPSS Across All UNDP Programming

SP Obj 2 + 3 + 4 | Integrated Outputs C.2 + C.5

The gap: MHPSS is still treated as a standalone programme by most UNDP Country Offices, rather than as a cross-cutting dimension of governance, climate, and crisis work.

Evidence base: Rwanda's triple integration (MHPSS + peacebuilding + livelihoods) produced outcomes that none achieved alone. The Burundi case demonstrated that MHPSS-trained police transformed community security. Climate eco-anxiety is measurable and affects programme outcomes.

Concrete opportunity: Develop a "MHPSS mainstreaming toolkit" for UNDP COs — a one-page MHPSS integration checklist per SP objective, with sample results language already aligned to IRRF codes. Target: MHPSS dimension in at least 1 programme per SP objective per regional bureau by 2027.

SP alignment: Integrated Output C.2 (Social Cohesion & Peace); C.5 (Multi-Dimensional Risk Management); Obj 4 (Healthy Planet — climate-mental health nexus).
📊

Build the Evidence Architecture

SP Obj 3 | All IRRF Outputs | C.2.1 → C.2.3

The gap: UNDP's strongest evidence (Rwanda, Nigeria Borno, Tajikistan) comes from partner evaluations (Interpeace, International Alert, BJPsych Open). UNDP does not yet have a systematic approach to generating and publishing its own MHPSS evidence.

What this means: Donors and partners cannot easily verify UNDP's impact, and UNDP cannot compellingly differentiate itself from NGO implementers who may have stronger published evidence bases.

Concrete opportunity: Design a lightweight MHPSS Outcome Monitoring Framework (MOMF) — 5 standardised indicators (PHQ-9/GAD-7, NCI social cohesion, VE vulnerability) that all MHPSS-integrated UNDP programmes collect, enabling a pooled evidence dataset across 27+ countries by 2027.

SP alignment: All IRRF outputs — rigorous project-level measurement is the prerequisite for reporting against IRRF indicators. UNDP's Monitoring Framework goes in the SP evidence architecture.
🤝

Formalise and Expand Strategic Partnerships

SP Accelerator: Finance + Gender | Integrated Output C.1

The gap: UNDP's current MHPSS partnerships are strong but largely bilateral (WHO, PBF, Interpeace) and project-based. There is no formal strategic framework for MHPSS partnerships that goes beyond individual programme collaboration.

Opportunities identified:
  • Brain Economy formal collaboration (EMEA, Rice University, WEF ecosystem): Build on the Sep 2025 Brain-Positive Innovations co-hosting into a structured collaboration. Concrete ask: co-develop a "Brain Capital for Fragile Contexts" indicator module alongside the existing 28-indicator Brain Capital Index — positioning UNDP as the equity and fragility voice in the global framework.
  • WHO Foundation (Catarina Mastellaro): Joint investment cases for LDCs — UNDP programme presence + WHO clinical standards + WHO Foundation financing convening power.
  • Wellcome Trust: Partnership on the Greentree Mental Health Acceleration Plan — UNDP contributes fragile context evidence; Wellcome funds longitudinal research.
  • UNITAR: Formalise the 2025 partnership on MHPSS in peacebuilding into a joint curriculum for the UNDP MHPSS Bootcamp and UNITAR's peacebuilding training portfolio.

SP alignment: Sustainable Finance Accelerator (B.3); Gender Accelerator (B.2) — women-led partnerships for MHPSS; Integrated Output C.1 (Local Governance & Communities).

What is the Brain Economy? (Click each pillar to expand)

🧠

Brain Capital

Skills + Health

Definition: Aggregate cognitive, emotional, and social capacities of people.

Includes: Educational attainment, creativity, problem-solving, resilience, mental health status.

Key insight: "Our collective brain power is a prosperity and national security asset."
💰

Investment Case

Mental health = economic strategy

Evidence: $1 invested = $15 return (Philippines, over 20 years). $39 return for community group therapy.

Shift: Mental health is an economic strategy, not just a social imperative.

Gap: Brain disorders cost more than heart disease or cancer globally, yet receive a fraction of the funding.
🎓

Skills & Innovation

Cognitive resilience for AI age

Focus: Human cognitive resilience and adaptability in the AI era.

Need: A "re-training revolution" for an AI-enhanced future.

Frontier: Neurotechnology and brain-computer interfaces creating new economic activity.
🌎

Global Policy

Brain capital in all policies

Strategy: "Brain capital in all policies" approach.

Frameworks: OECD Neuroscience-Inspired Policy Initiative, WHO Global Brain Health Action Plan, G7/G20 agendas.

Measurement: Global Brain Capital Dashboard with 3 pillars: Drivers, Health, Skills.
⚖️

Equity in Fragility

UNDP's unique contribution

UNDP's key question: "How do we ensure that the brain economy does not remain the privilege of stability -- but becomes a vehicle for equity in crisis-affected and fragile settings?"

UNDP concept note: $110K Phase I, 12 months, enabling fragile contexts to participate in global brain economy conversations.

Outputs: 1 public synthesis brief, 2-4 learning notes, curated insights from the field.

Training Approaches -- Key Models

Cascading ToT

Train master trainers who then train local facilitators. Used in South Sudan (150 volunteers), Yemen (4,750 education staff), Turkmenistan (social workers, police, teachers).

Digital-First

Online platforms for scale. Turkmenistan (bootcamp.com.tm in 3 languages), Ukraine (Diia.Education), UNDP MHPSS Bootcamp (global, self-paced with certificate).

Integrated Curriculum

MHPSS embedded in broader programming. Iraq (MHPSS + vocational), Philippines (MHPSS + women peacebuilding), Rwanda (MHPSS + peacebuilding + livelihoods).

Government System Embedding

Training government staff directly. Iraq (25 ministry reps for Al-Hol), Nepal (national MH strategy alignment), The Gambia (national mental health policy).

Faith & Community Based

Leveraging trusted community structures. Somalia (faith leaders + mental health), Rwanda (Anglican Church sociotherapy), Nigeria (community counselling).

Youth Co-Design

Young people as co-designers, not just beneficiaries. Kyrgyzstan (12 youth-led action plans), Bangladesh (120 Mental Health First Aid Responders), Philippines (18 Muslim youth leaders).

    Strategic Recommendations — UNDP MHPSS 2026–2029

    Eight strategic directions drawn from field experience, evidence review, and analytical research. Click any pillar to expand for rationale, specific actions, and success metrics.

    Grounded in UNDP country programme documentation (2021–2025), IASC MHPSS guidelines, SP 2026-2029, and peer-reviewed evidence.

    1 🗣️

    Complementary Framing

    Match language to audience — keep MHPSS for technical partners; use "brain economy" and "resilience" for finance and political audiences

    Rationale: "MHPSS" is an internationally recognized IASC/WHO term — dropping it would sever technical credibility. Different audiences, however, respond to different framings. This is not rebranding; it is strategic communication.

    Specific Actions:
    • Develop a 1-page "Language Bridge" guide for country offices (MHPSS ↔ brain economy ↔ resilience)
    • Brief HQ communications team on audience-specific terminology
    • Tag all dashboard content with applicable framing (technical / financing / political)
    Success Metric: Country offices report increased donor engagement when using adapted framing in 3+ new proposals (2026–2027)

    2 📐

    Evidence Architecture

    Standardised outcome monitoring across 27+ country programmes — from fragmented reporting to unified M&E

    Rationale: UNDP has strong individual studies (BJPsych Open peer-reviewed, Interpeace Rwanda evaluation, PBF Gambia) but no cross-country M&E framework. Without it, the portfolio cannot demonstrate cumulative impact to funders.

    Specific Actions:
    • Adopt IASC MSP Indicator Bank (2024) as the baseline measurement framework
    • Define 3–5 mandatory core indicators for all MHPSS-funded country programmes
    • Publish a biennial MHPSS evidence synthesis (first edition: 2026)
    • Partner with WHO and academia for third-party validation
    Success Metric: ≥10 country offices using standardized indicators by end 2027; first synthesis published

    3 💰

    Financing via Brain Economy

    Position UNDP as the equity and fragility voice in a $5T global agenda — unlocking new donor and DFI financing streams

    Rationale: The Brain Economy agenda (WEF, EMEA, Rice University, G7) is growing rapidly. UNDP's comparative advantage — crisis contexts, fragile states, equity focus — is currently absent from mainstream brain capital frameworks. This is a financing gap and an opportunity.

    Specific Actions:
    • Develop a "Brain Capital for Fragile Contexts" position paper and indicator module
    • Brief bilateral donors (Netherlands, EU, Japan) using brain economy ROI language
    • Engage development finance institutions (DFIs) and sovereign wealth funds
    • Submit to G7 Mental Health Financing working group (2026)
    Success Metric: At least 1 new financing instrument or partnership secured using brain economy framing by 2027

    4 🧭

    Operationalize Across SP Objectives

    Move MHPSS from a standalone niche to a cross-cutting accelerator across all four SP 2026-2029 objectives

    Rationale: MHPSS is currently mapped primarily to C.2 (social cohesion) and A.3.2 (stabilization/reintegration). This undercounts MHPSS's contribution to prosperity, governance, and climate — and limits CO project integration.

    Specific Actions:
    • Map concrete MHPSS activities to each of the 4 SP objectives with worked examples
    • Include MHPSS in regional bureau SP implementation guidance notes
    • Create a "MHPSS Mainstreaming Checklist" for project design teams
    • Pilot integration in 3 non-crisis COs (e.g., climate, digital, governance programmes)
    Success Metric: MHPSS referenced as a cross-cutting element in ≥50% of CO SP-aligned project documents by 2027

    5 🧠

    Deepen Brain Economy Engagement

    Build on co-hosting the Brain-Positive Innovations session (Sep 2025) into structured collaboration on brain capital measurement for crisis contexts

    Rationale: UNDP co-hosted the Brain-Positive Innovations in Crisis Recovery and Development session (Sep 2025) with EMEA and Rice University — establishing a platform for deeper collaboration. The 28-indicator Brain Capital Index currently does not include a fragile-contexts module. UNDP is positioned to lead this gap.

    Specific Actions:
    • Formalize collaboration with EMEA and Rice University on a structured research agenda
    • Co-develop a "Brain Capital for Fragile Contexts" indicator module (target: 5–7 crisis-adapted indicators)
    • Contribute fragile-state data from 27+ UNDP country programmes to the global Brain Capital Index
    • Explore joint publications and advocacy at WEF/G20 events
    Success Metric: Formal collaboration agreement or joint publication with EMEA/Rice University by end 2026

    6 🏛️

    Strengthen Institutional Architecture

    Dedicated MHPSS capacity at regional bureau level — beyond HQ-led SURGE deployments

    Rationale: Current MHPSS capacity is concentrated at HQ (Crisis Bureau) with Dutch-funded SURGE deployments. Country offices requesting technical support face delays. Without standing regional capacity, MHPSS remains fragile and event-driven.

    Specific Actions:
    • Establish MHPSS focal points in at least 3 UNDP regional bureaux by mid-2026
    • Secure multi-year core funding for a global MHPSS team (min. 3 FTEs)
    • Formalize the Community of Practice (259+ members) with structured governance
    • Create a regional peer-learning mechanism between MHPSS focal points
    Success Metric: Measurable reduction in CO technical support response time; regional focal points operational in ≥3 bureaux by 2026

    7 🚀

    Scale Proven Innovation Models

    Systematically adopt and adapt evidence-based field models across country offices — from ad hoc to portfolio-wide

    Rationale: Field teams have developed and/or deployed proven models — Task-Sharing (WHO mhGAP), Problem Management Plus (PM+), Narrative Exposure Therapy (NET), group interpersonal therapy — but adoption is uneven. A structured scale-up approach would multiply impact without proportional cost.

    Specific Actions:
    • Create a "MHPSS Model Adoption Toolkit" with step-by-step adaptation guides for each model
    • Partner with WHO mhGAP faculty for Training of Trainers (ToT) programme
    • Document ≥3 new adaptation case studies annually from field offices
    • Add "innovation model" cards for all 27+ country programmes to the MHPSS in Action tab
    Success Metric: ≥5 additional COs implementing a standardized evidence-based model by 2027; ≥3 documented adaptations annually

    8 📣

    Enhance Communications & Visibility

    One flagship annual MHPSS report + systematic country office story capture — building UNDP's narrative architecture

    Rationale: UNDP does strong MHPSS work but has fragmented visibility. Individual country stories exist but are not linked to a global narrative. Donors and leadership need a coherent, evidence-backed story about the portfolio's scale and impact.

    Specific Actions:
    • Publish "UNDP MHPSS Annual Review" (concise, ~12 pages, donor-friendly, design-led)
    • Implement a simple CO story capture template (quarterly, 2-page max)
    • Build a media pipeline: 2–3 flagship stories per year at HQ level
    • Brief ASG-level leadership quarterly with a 1-page MHPSS dashboard summary
    • Create a public-facing version of this dashboard for external audiences
    Success Metric: Annual Review published by Q3 2026; ≥10 CO stories captured quarterly; measurable increase in MHPSS media mentions

    How to use these recommendations: Each pillar is independent but mutually reinforcing. Start with Pillars 1 and 6 (framing + institutional base) to create conditions for all others. Pillars 2 and 7 (evidence + innovation) build the credibility case. Pillars 3, 4, 5 (financing + SP integration + Brain Economy) unlock resources. Pillar 8 sustains momentum through visibility.
